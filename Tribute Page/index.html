Dr. Norman Borlaug

  • Led initiatives worldwide that contributed to the extensive increases in agricultural production termed the Green Revolution.
  • Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970 for his contributions to world peace through increasing food supply.
  • Developed high-yielding, disease-resistant wheat varieties that significantly boosted food production in countries like Mexico, India, and Pakistan.
  • Often called "the father of the Green Revolution" for his work in transforming agriculture.
  • Recipient of the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the Congressional Gold Medal, and numerous other honors.
  • Founded the World Food Prize, which honors individuals who have advanced human development by improving the quality, quantity, or availability of food in the world.
  • Worked extensively in Latin America, South Asia, and Africa to implement agricultural advancements and combat hunger.
  • Authored numerous scientific papers and books on agriculture and food security.
  • Inspired a generation of agronomists and researchers dedicated to improving global food security.
